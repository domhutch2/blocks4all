# Catheter Troubleshooting

For anaesthetic staff managing nerve catheter problems. Based on NUTH Guideline for Peripheral Nerve Block Catheters in Adults (v2, February 2024).

:::warning
These troubleshooting guides are for **anaesthetic staff only** — not for non-anaesthetic medical or nursing staff on the wards.
:::

## Patient in pain

1. Check the cutaneous block with ethyl chloride spray; assess motor block (or inspiratory spirometry for rib fractures)
2. Ensure no disconnections and the infusion rate is correct and running
3. **Is the catheter still in situ?**
   - **No** → Re-site or prescribe alternative analgesia and inform pain team
   - **Yes** → Assess the block:
4. **Is there an adequate block?**
   - **Inadequate** → Bolus 10–20 mL of 0.25% levobupivacaine. Re-site if ineffective.
   - **Dense block but inadequate cover** → Consider bolus. Consider adding alternate analgesia. For rib fractures: consider siting another PVB at a different level, or **switching to an epidural** if further coverage needed.

## Leaking around the insertion site

This is common — insertion needles are wider than the catheter.

1. **Is the catheter at the same depth as insertion?**
   - **No** → Re-site or prescribe alternative analgesia and inform pain team
   - **Yes** → Check for subcutaneous collection:
2. **Subcutaneous collection present?**
   - **Yes** → Re-site or prescribe alternative analgesia and inform pain team
   - **No** → Apply Dermabond to the insertion site and a new dressing

## Blocked catheter

1. **Check the giving set** — is it clamped or incorrectly connected?
   - **Yes** → Unclamp, check NRFit connections are correct, continue infusion
2. **Check the catheter** — is it kinked or stretched?
   - **Kinked** → Check aseptically under the dressing, unkink. Consider sterile Steri-Strip to avoid further kinks.
   - **Stretched** → If there is length, cut the stretched segment with sterile scissors and reconnect.
3. **Blocked catheter** — attempt to inject 0.9% sodium chloride (aseptic technique):
   - **Able to flush** → Check infusion is running. If yes, resolved.
   - **Unable to flush** → Plan to re-site or prescribe alternative analgesia

## Signs of LA toxicity

:::danger
If signs of LA toxicity (metallic taste, confusion, dizziness, tinnitus):
:::

1. **Stop the infusion immediately**
2. ABC assessment. Consider moving patient to a monitored area (e.g. Recovery). Inform pain team.
3. **Any CVS/neurological impairment?**
   - **Yes** → Follow the [AAGBI Management of Severe LA Toxicity guideline](./la-toxicity.md)
   - **No** → Check for blood on aspiration:
4. **Blood on aspiration?**
   - **Yes** → Withdraw catheter by 1–2 cm and aspirate again. If no blood, restart infusion in a monitored area. If still blood, re-site or prescribe alternative. Inform pain team.
   - **No** → Once symptoms resolve, restart infusion at a **lower rate** with continual monitoring. Give instructions to stop if symptoms recur.

## Disconnections

**Disconnect proximal to filter** (between filter and nerve catheter)?
- **Yes** + witnessed/informed immediately → Spray catheter with 0.5% chlorhexidine, cut the tip by 1 cm, restart infusion
- **Yes** + not witnessed immediately + visibly soiled → Re-site or prescribe alternative, inform pain team
- **Yes** + not witnessed immediately + not visibly soiled → Spray with 0.5% chlorhexidine, cut tip by 1 cm, restart infusion, inform pain team
- **No** (distal to filter) → Change the giving set and reconnect to the filter

*Keep the pump sterile and re-use if the catheter is re-sited.*
