# LA Toxicity & Lipid Rescue

## Recognition

Local anaesthetic systemic toxicity (LAST) can present within minutes of injection or up to 60 minutes later.

### Early signs (CNS — usually first)

- Perioral tingling / numbness
- Metallic taste
- Tinnitus / auditory disturbance
- Dizziness, lightheadedness
- Agitation, confusion
- Visual disturbance

### Progression (CNS → CVS)

- Tremor → seizures → loss of consciousness
- Bradycardia, conduction block
- Hypotension
- Ventricular arrhythmias → cardiac arrest

:::danger Important
**Cardiovascular collapse can occur without preceding CNS symptoms** — especially with bupivacaine. Bupivacaine cardiotoxicity is particularly resistant to standard resuscitation.
:::

## Immediate management

### 1. Stop injecting

- Stop LA injection immediately
- Call for help

### 2. Airway and seizure management

- Manage airway — 100% oxygen
- If seizing: **midazolam 1–2 mg IV** or propofol in small doses
- Avoid large doses of propofol (cardio-depressant)
- Intubate if necessary

### 3. Intralipid 20% (lipid emulsion rescue)

:::tip Intralipid Protocol (AAGBI guideline)
**BOLUS**: 1.5 mL/kg IV over 1 minute

**INFUSION**: 15 mL/kg/hr IV

**If cardiovascular instability persists after 5 minutes:**
- Repeat bolus (up to 2 further boluses at 5-minute intervals)
- Double the infusion rate to 30 mL/kg/hr

**Maximum cumulative dose**: 12 mL/kg

**For a 70 kg patient:**
- Bolus = 105 mL
- Infusion = 1050 mL/hr
:::

### 4. Cardiac arrest management

- Start CPR — **prolonged resuscitation may be required** (LA cardiotoxicity can be reversed)
- Avoid vasopressin, calcium channel blockers, beta-blockers, and further local anaesthetic (e.g. lidocaine for arrhythmia)
- **Adrenaline**: use small doses (< 1 mcg/kg) — large boluses may worsen outcome
- **Amiodarone** for ventricular arrhythmias (not lidocaine)
- Consider cardiopulmonary bypass / ECMO if available and arrest is refractory

## Intralipid — practical points

- **Location**: Know where Intralipid is stored in your department — it should be immediately accessible in all areas where regional anaesthesia is performed
- Intralipid comes as 20% solution in 250 mL or 500 mL bags
- Can be given through a standard IV giving set — does not need a central line
- Start Intralipid early — do not wait for cardiac arrest
- Continue Intralipid even if ROSC is achieved — re-arrest is possible

## Prevention

- **Ultrasound guidance** for all peripheral nerve blocks
- **Aspirate before every injection** and inject incrementally (3–5 mL aliquots)
- **Use the minimum effective dose** of LA
- **Adrenaline as a marker**: heart rate increase or T-wave changes suggest intravascular injection
- **Know the maximum dose** before drawing up
- **Monitor the patient** during and for 30 minutes after injection
- **Have Intralipid immediately available** wherever blocks are performed

## Reporting

All cases of LAST should be reported to:
- Local incident reporting system
- National reporting: [AAGBI LAST registry](https://www.nationalauditprojects.org.uk)
