# LA Safe Dosing & Calculations

## Maximum recommended doses

| Local Anaesthetic | Max Dose (mg/kg) | Max Dose (mg/kg) with Adrenaline | Absolute Max (70 kg) | Onset | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Lidocaine** | 3 | 7 | 200 / 500 mg | Fast (5 min) | 1–2 hr |
| **Bupivacaine** | 2 | 2 | 150 mg | Moderate (15 min) | 4–8 hr |
| **Levobupivacaine** | 2 | 2 | 150 mg | Moderate (15 min) | 4–8 hr |
| **Ropivacaine** | 3 | 3 | 200 mg | Moderate (15 min) | 6–12 hr |
| **Prilocaine** | 6 | 8 | 400 / 600 mg | Fast (5 min) | 1.5–3 hr |

:::warning
These are maximum doses — not target doses. Use the minimum effective dose. Doses are additive when mixing agents.
:::

## Quick dose calculator

### Ropivacaine (most common for nerve blocks)

| Concentration | Volume containing 1 mg/mL | 20 mL contains | 30 mL contains |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0.2%** | 2 mg/mL | 40 mg | 60 mg |
| **0.375%** | 3.75 mg/mL | 75 mg | 112.5 mg |
| **0.5%** | 5 mg/mL | 100 mg | 150 mg |
| **0.75%** | 7.5 mg/mL | 150 mg | 225 mg |

**Example**: 70 kg patient, max ropivacaine = 210 mg. That's 42 mL of 0.5% or 56 mL of 0.375%.

### Bupivacaine / Levobupivacaine

| Concentration | mg/mL | 20 mL contains | 30 mL contains |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0.25%** | 2.5 mg/mL | 50 mg | 75 mg |
| **0.375%** | 3.75 mg/mL | 75 mg | 112.5 mg |
| **0.5%** | 5 mg/mL | 100 mg | 150 mg |

### Lidocaine

| Concentration | mg/mL | 10 mL contains | 20 mL contains |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0.5%** | 5 mg/mL | 50 mg | 100 mg |
| **1%** | 10 mg/mL | 100 mg | 200 mg |
| **2%** | 20 mg/mL | 200 mg | 400 mg |

## Percentage to mg/mL conversion

**1% = 10 mg/mL**

So: 0.25% = 2.5 mg/mL, 0.5% = 5 mg/mL, 0.75% = 7.5 mg/mL, 2% = 20 mg/mL

## Key principles

- **Calculate the dose in mg**, not volume — a "safe" volume of a high concentration can still be toxic
- **Doses are cumulative** — if using multiple blocks or supplementing with local infiltration, add all LA together
- **Patient factors that reduce safe dose**: extremes of age, hepatic impairment, cardiac disease, low body weight, pregnancy (more sensitive to cardiotoxicity), hypoalbuminaemia
- **Ropivacaine** has a better safety profile than bupivacaine (less cardiotoxic, lower CNS toxicity threshold)
- **Intravascular injection** causes toxicity at much lower doses than subcutaneous — always aspirate and use incremental injection
- **Ultrasound guidance** reduces the risk of intravascular injection

## Adjuvants

| Adjuvant | Dose | Effect | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adrenaline** | 1:200,000 (5 mcg/mL) | Prolongs block, reduces absorption, marker for intravascular injection | Avoid in digital blocks (debated), end-artery territories |
| **Dexamethasone** (perineural) | 4 mg | Prolongs block by 6–8 hours | Off-label; equivalent evidence for IV route |
| **Dexamethasone** (IV) | 4–8 mg | Prolongs block by 4–6 hours | Give at time of block |
| **Clonidine** | 1 mcg/kg (max 150 mcg) | Modest prolongation | Can cause bradycardia, sedation, hypotension |
